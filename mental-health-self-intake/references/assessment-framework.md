# Assessment Framework

Use this reference to support a Chinese self-intake conversation. It is a triage and self-understanding framework, not a diagnostic manual.

## Non-Negotiable Limits

- Do not give a definitive diagnosis.
- Do not prescribe, change, stop, or compare medications.
- Do not promise confidentiality, recovery, or safety.
- Do not tell a user with imminent risk to handle it alone.
- Do not provide detailed self-harm methods or lethality comparisons.
- Do not continue an ordinary questionnaire when immediate safety is unresolved.

Use language such as:

- "这不是确诊，但从你描述的信息看，可能需要专业评估..."
- "目前更重要的是安全和支持，而不是先把问题命名。"
- "我可以帮你整理风险和下一步，但不能替代咨询师或精神科医生。"

## Risk Levels

### Low

Typical signs:

- Distress is present but manageable.
- No current self-harm/suicide thoughts, no harm-to-others risk.
- Functioning is mildly affected or still mostly intact.
- Has support, coping options, and willingness to seek help if symptoms worsen.

Response:

- Offer self-monitoring, routine stabilization, social support, and optional counseling.
- Suggest professional support if symptoms persist, worsen, or impair functioning.

### Medium

Typical signs:

- Passive death wishes, vague self-harm thoughts, or increasing hopelessness without plan/means/intent.
- Noticeable impairment in sleep, appetite, study/work, relationships, or self-care.
- Limited support, recent major stressor, substance use, trauma reminders, or prior mental-health history.
- User is uncertain whether they can cope, but can agree to short-term safety steps.

Response:

- Recommend contacting a trusted person and arranging professional help soon.
- Ask direct follow-up questions about plan, means, timing, prior attempts, intoxication, and ability to stay safe.
- Suggest crisis hotline or urgent assessment if risk escalates.

### High

Typical signs:

- Current suicidal ideation with some intent, planning, preparation, or access to means.
- Recent self-harm or suicide attempt.
- Inability to commit to safety for the next 24 hours.
- Severe agitation, intoxication, impulsivity, command hallucinations, psychosis-like symptoms linked to unsafe behavior.
- Harm-to-others thoughts with target, plan, means, or escalating loss of control.
- Severe abuse/coercive control where the user may be in danger.

Response:

- Pause ordinary intake.
- Urge immediate contact with local crisis services, emergency department, or emergency services.
- Encourage staying with or contacting a trusted nearby person.
- Encourage reducing access to means in a general, non-method-specific way.
- Continue only with grounding, connection to help, and immediate next steps.

### Emergency

Typical signs:

- Attempt in progress, injury, overdose, severe intoxication, weapon or lethal means currently present with intent.
- User says they will act now or very soon.
- Severe medical symptoms or loss of orientation.
- Immediate threat to another person.

Response:

- Tell the user to contact emergency services now or ask someone nearby to do it.
- Mainland China: `110`/`120` for immediate danger or medical emergency; `12356` for psychological assistance where available.
- United States: call/text/chat `988` for mental-health crisis support; call emergency services for immediate physical danger.
- Other locations: local emergency number, nearest emergency department, local crisis line.
- Do not continue assessment before emergency help is engaged.

## Diagnostic Direction Hypotheses

Frame these as "可能需要专业评估的方向", based only on reported signs. Include differential uncertainty.

- Depressive direction: persistent low mood, anhedonia, hopelessness, low energy, sleep/appetite changes, guilt, concentration problems, impaired function.
- Anxiety direction: excessive worry, panic symptoms, avoidance, physical arousal, reassurance seeking, fear of uncertainty.
- Trauma-related direction: intrusive memories, nightmares, avoidance, hypervigilance, emotional numbing, startle response after trauma or chronic threat.
- Obsessive-compulsive direction: intrusive unwanted thoughts and repetitive behaviors or mental rituals used to reduce distress.
- Bipolar-spectrum direction: episodes of elevated or very irritable mood with decreased need for sleep, increased energy, racing thoughts, impulsivity, grandiosity, or risky behavior.
- Psychosis-related direction: hallucinations, fixed false beliefs, severe thought disorganization, feeling controlled, or marked detachment from shared reality.
- Substance-related direction: distress or functional impairment connected to alcohol/drug use, medication misuse, intoxication, withdrawal, cravings, or failed attempts to cut down.
- Somatic/medical contribution: new severe physical symptoms, endocrine/neurological signs, medication effects, postpartum changes, sleep deprivation, pain, or chronic illness may require medical evaluation.
- Adjustment/stress direction: symptoms closely follow identifiable stressors such as loss, relationship rupture, workplace/school pressure, migration, financial strain, or family conflict.

When evidence is insufficient, say what is missing rather than forcing a category.

## High-Yield Follow-Up Questions

Ask only the questions needed for the next decision.

- "过去两周，这种状态大概有多少天出现？每天持续多久？"
- "它最影响你的哪一块：睡眠、吃饭、学习/工作、人际、自我照顾，还是安全感？"
- "你有没有出现过不想活、想伤害自己，或者觉得自己可能控制不住的念头？"
- "如果有这些念头，有没有具体计划、时间、方式、准备行为，或手边已经有相关物品？"
- "你最近是否伤害过自己、尝试过自杀，或明显增加饮酒/用药/使用物质？"
- "你现在身边有没有一个可以马上联系的人？"
- "有没有听见别人听不到的声音、强烈觉得有人要害你，或感觉自己被控制？"
- "最近有没有一段时间睡很少却精力很高、话变多、冲动花钱或做高风险决定？"
- "你以前做过咨询、看过精神科、用过药或住过院吗？哪些有帮助，哪些没有？"

## Protective Referral Modifiers

Be more conservative and referral-oriented when the user is:

- Under 18, especially with self-harm, abuse, bullying, family violence, or school refusal.
- Pregnant, postpartum, or caring for an infant while reporting severe mood changes or intrusive harm thoughts.
- An older adult with isolation, bereavement, cognitive changes, chronic pain, or access to lethal means.
- Experiencing domestic violence, sexual violence, coercive control, stalking, trafficking, or unsafe housing.
- Reporting psychosis-like symptoms, mania-like symptoms, severe substance use, eating disorder medical risk, or severe self-neglect.
- Lacking local support or unable to access routine care.

## Suggested Response Styles

For low or medium distress:

- Validate briefly.
- Summarize facts.
- Give risk level with reasons.
- Offer practical next steps and professional support options.

For high or emergency risk:

- Use short sentences.
- Focus on safety, connection, and emergency resources.
- Avoid analysis-heavy explanations.
- Keep the user oriented to the next concrete action.
